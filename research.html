My Research Explorations

Consumption of high sugar diets (HSD) leads to metabolic disorders in females. Effects of HSD on the adult stem cells have not been elucidated in depth. In this study, we used Drosophila melanogaster to investigate how HSD affects ovarian Germline Stem Cells (GSCs) via adipocyte communication. Our findings revealed that high sugar diets cause premature loss of GSCs, independent of niche composition. Notably, reducing histone acetylation in adipocytes restored normal GSC differentiation. Our current focus is identifying the interorgan communication factor involved. Overall, this study sheds light on how altered adipocyte metabolism under high sugar conditions affects GSC dynamics.

In my study, I investigated the interaction between the C-terminal of nascent protein chains and the ribosome exit tunnel, using variants of superfolder GFP (sfGFP) with C-terminal mutations (CTK, CTA, CTD). These modifications were introduced to understand their impact on protein folding. The study involved cloning mutated sfGFPs, followed by affinity purification and quantitative analysis of their refolding kinetics compared to wild-type sfGFP. The results revealed significant insights into how C-terminal variations influence protein folding, offering potential implications for understanding protein behavior in both normal physiological and disease states within cells.

In my research, I targeted Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor 1 (S1PR1), a key player in lymphocyte egress and Multiple Sclerosis pathogenesis, for molecular modeling as a potential therapeutic target. I performed virtual screening of 100 molecules, selecting candidates based on structural similarity to a specific anthraquinone compound. The selected molecules showed promising binding energies to S1PR1. Molecular docking revealed detailed interactions at the atomic level, suggesting potential for drug development. These findings, supported by in-silico ADMET analysis, provide a foundation for future in-vitro and in-vivo studies, advancing the understanding of S1PR1 modulation in Multiple Sclerosis treatment.
